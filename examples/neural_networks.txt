Neural networks are computational models inspired by biological neural systems.

Deep learning uses multiple layers of neural networks to learn hierarchical representations.

Backpropagation is an algorithm for training neural networks by computing gradients.

Gradient descent requires backpropagation to update network weights.

Convolutional neural networks specialize in processing grid-like data such as images.

Recurrent neural networks maintain internal state to process sequential data.

Transformers replaced recurrent architectures in many natural language processing tasks.

Attention mechanisms allow models to focus on relevant parts of the input.

The transformer architecture depends on self-attention mechanisms.

Large language models are transformers trained on massive text corpora.

BERT introduced bidirectional training for language understanding tasks.

GPT models use autoregressive generation for text completion.

Fine-tuning adapts pre-trained models to specific downstream tasks.

Transfer learning leverages knowledge from one domain to improve performance in another.
